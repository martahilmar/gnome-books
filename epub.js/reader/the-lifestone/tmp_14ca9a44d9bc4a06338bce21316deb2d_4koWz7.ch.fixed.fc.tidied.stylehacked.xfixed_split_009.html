Chapter 4

The creatures had carpeted the dead dusty land from the base of the mountains to the first of the dead torched corpses. Thousands of them scattered across the mighty plain, then the gaps that they created in their number filled in as more of the minions poured along the black road, they spread out, much like spilled blood on a hard floor. Nailstead, I knew, would be somewhere in the mountain range, watching and directing his army. He would pour more and more creatures onto the plain, filling it up, creating a sea of evil bloated bodies, which would break like waves onto our defenses.

Though the tactic was not very original or inventive, it was potent with the possibility of destroying any resistance that we might place before it. Shear weight of numbers would force us to retreat, and defensive positions would become useless to us as creatures closed in upon them. Killing thousands would only create space for more to file out. I had no way of knowing how many or how large the force facing us could be, all I knew was that each of them opposed me and mine wanting to see us gone, left decimated and destroyed. All that I knew for certain was that Nailstead has been in those mountains for longer than twenty years, continually building, planning, forming and constructing his army. Drawing to him all that had been disillusioned or turned, all that felt natural evil to be the best over life and freedom for all.

Every life form that feared normalcy had hoarded to Nailstead's never ending call. Creature from darkness had swarmed up from the center of the world traveling the deep hidden passageways which led from the underworld, always seeking out and joining forces with Nailstead. He would be able to summon a seemingly endless number of minions to fight his war, whilst we the defenders, would be restricted by the number of troops that were available. We had called and summoned throughout the lands, thousands had responded, but thousands against possibly millions did not seem to be viable, yet we would fight for our right to live, as Nailstead would fight for his right to rule and to see us die.

My troops were in place ready to rebuff the first surges. Ariel spear throwers and archers would dive to attack the rear of the ranks on the crowded plain, trying to creating a buffer zone of dead creatures. Fire cartons would be released to plummet to the ground there to scatter and coat, each filled with burning tar, hopefully adding to the confusion and creating incinerated death as the forces start to maneuver. I look up seeing only clouds forming in the high sky, my Magi have woven their spells well, the camouflage is perfect, the sky warriors have returned unseen to wait for the ideal and anointed moment. Soon the sky would explode in war cries as wave upon wave of sky riders surged out of the camouflage directly into the marching heart of Nailstead's attacking force, causing their own form of devastation.

Soon the time would be upon us, the last hopes entered into, the battle engaged fully. Many would die on both sides, slaughtered to a cause that is both pointless and disturbing to the will of man, and elf alike. Yet it was a battle which must be engaged in if life as it is known to us is to exist. Without the battle being fought, if life as we know it with all its hopes and flaws was to remain constant, then this battle was a necessary consequence of those desires and must be both fought and won.

 

My thoughts wondered back through time as I waited for the battle to commence. I remember being a child, small in physical stature which made the rearing crevice seem even higher and therefore more intimidating. I walked on, edged forward by an invisible touch, my path lit by burning sconces set regular into the wall on alternating sides of the passageway. Before me was another door, not the sort of door that this place implied should be infront of me. It was thin, weedy looking, as though a slight shove would push it from its hinges to the floor.

The wood looked frail, eaten into by thousands of termites, over thousands of years, I hesitated to touch it less it crumble before me. With no option but to push on, and with the unseen hand guiding my presence, I reached forward to gently touch the ancient wood. My fingers tingled; the feeling crept up my arm before I could pull my hand away. Nothing happened, I just stood there studying the wood, listening for anything, ready to run, but terrified of what I would find if I ran. I had doubts as to whether I could run if I really wanted to, doubts that seemed set in stone and beyond my ability to fight even were I to decide to resist the call and push towards and through that door.

Before me the door swung open and a light flicked on, driving away shadows caused by the flickering fire held safely in the wall mounted sconces behind me. My own shadow had briefly been visible infront of me, it seemed to my childish mind to be telling me to get out, leave, go now whilst you can, run boy run! I ignored it, I had no other choice, I had to believe that I had been brought to this place for a reason, a good reason or so I hoped. I restrained my desires and stood looking forwards accepting my fate, accepting the guidance being given to me.

I walked slowly towards the now open door, as my shadow fell further forwards I noticed that it begin to vanish as it passed through the door. Beyond the frame I could see exactly nothing, not even my shadow. No light burst on to illuminate the way. I closed, my fear building, two steps to go still no light, one step, still no light, then I had reached the frame, I hesitate with my foot lifted as though to step through into the dark. Even this close there was nothing to see, the door seemed to be the entry to nowhere.

If I put my foot down on the other side of the frame would it continue to go down, past the floor level, tipping me forwards into a never ending plummet. Or would there be sound but invisible ground for my foot to rest on? There was only one way that I could ever know, but it took a certain kind of courage, a bravery that I was not certain that I contained. Slowly I lowered my foot, whilst grasping each side of the stone door frame tightly lest I plunge forward, at least there would be my hands and their grip to save me. My foot found firm ground beyond the doorway; much to my relief, I step further in letting go of the frame reluctantly.

Lights exploded around me for the briefest of time then they vanished; my body tingled from the nails on my toes to the tips of my longest hair on my head. The feeling was encompassing, every cell of my body felt its draw and power. I was still standing in pitch darkness. The floor below me was invisible though my toes told me that it was there. My hands waved about me, trying to feel something, anything, they failed.

"Welcome boy, you have finally arrived I see!"

The voice echoed all around me, sounding hollow and eerie to my ears, and I wondered how could the speaker see anything, I couldn't! Lightening flashed from behind me exploding into a brilliant white light that was tinged with electric blue, it spread across the surface of the rock walls surrounding me, walls that I was stood facing. To my surprise the wall vanishes revealing a magnificent view out over the distant plain and on to the mountains beyond, from where I have been told that I walked out of. I cannot remember anything prior to being met by the guard and then taken from there onward to this point, this meeting.

The air shimmered briefly before clearing once more, the view remained the same, but now I got the feeling that there was something between it and me, something invisible, but there all the same. I turned away from the view wanting to look towards where I had heard the voice come from, and where the lightening had originated from.

The room has been illuminated by the creation of the wall into a window. Now visible to me and sitting in a stone chair looking much like a statue herself, was a woman. She I knew had to be the one to which I had been brought, the witch, the Morganith, the wise woman of the mountains, she that had many names, all equally powerful, though others being more ancient than the name that is now used to describe her.

I stand upright, my spine pulled straight, a lowly boy child before a creature that has both extreme age and wisdom at her command. Let alone the power to turn stone walls into open airy windows, a power to be respected, if not feared, a power not to be challenged by me, but accepted as right and proper in this place.

"Why boy do you think that you have the right to replace the heir to the rights of ruler?"

"I don't" I mumbled shocked at her question, I have no wish to de-throne anyone, let alone the rightful heir to anything.

"Speak up child!"

I repeat my statement, this time with more force and determination in the simple words.

"I DON'T!"

She looked at me a cracking of skin across her brittle face. In any other person I would have thought that they were smiling, but in her, the action seemed more reminiscent of a parchment page ripping. Still she fixed her eyes on me, still she studied my presence as though trying to read my deepest thoughts.

The milky ness of old age coated her eyeballs, adding to the terrifying effect that her demeanor created as I looked into her face.

"That is good, very good!" Her voice crackled.

I was in no doubt that she was ancient and powerful, no doubt at all, it oozed from her, it swelled her body as it filled her eyes which burned with her power in evidence.

"You will make a great leader for our people, exactly as I have foreseen! You will be the power that will first unify and eventually over see the end of our way of life."

What she said that I would do seemed to be contradictive, I nearly decided to challenge her but just before I spoke she raised herself from the stone throne on which she had been sitting.

"Walk with me boy, come learn of the future that is yours by right, understand, see the lands that you and you alone shall hold dominion over. Come boy be brave, become the true King."

She led me across the room towards the open vista of the rolling plain and beyond. Something shimmered between us and the drop off, a slim upright of granite appeared.

Sitting on top of this granite up thrust, resting safely in a depression clearly ground out to fit it, was a round slightly glowing object. She, the witch, led me towards the object, as we closed it changed, clearing to form a liquid crystal, which changed and altered continually, forming and reforming it's shape. Held in place by an invisible barrier, a barrier created of nothing. The ball looked to me as though it were constructed of crystal clear water, living moving water, water without a coast or river bed to meet and travel upon. Water balanced upon a rock, held and waiting through time for my arrival. The thought frightened me because it foretold off a destiny about which I knew nothing, a destiny I was not certain that I wanted to understand or accept.

Little did or could I know then as I gazed into the crystal, exactly how much and for how long this entity would have dominion over my life. I would realise only when as a full grown man and ruler of my kingdom that it would be this crystal and the power it contained, which would aide me to bring peace to my world and control the evil that would threaten it.

This stone of water that the witch was introducing me to, could do all of this and much more. Many years from now, at the eve of a dire battle, when all could be lost forever, this stone of liquid would fill my mind, create in it a plan of action and if I used it correctly, it might save all good life, whilst protecting, by binding away all evil. Time would pass slowly, but the crystal so long as it is protected, would continue on. Carrying its bound creatures, in a world of living, but restrained, onward through the coming centuries.

"This is the one crystal, the only one of its kind known to man or elf. This is to be the Lifestone or stone of light," The witch informed me.

I, a child looked upon the oscillating power in awe, feeling it reach out and connect with my thoughts. The stone sucked at my mind, pushed aside the tendrils of mental confusion and childhood, entering into my body and soul. Not exactly taking control, but engendering a feeling of wellbeing and balance. I fought the entry at first, I struggled hard against it, but I relaxed as the witch lightly rested her bony hand on my shoulders, reassuring me.

 

The field of conflict lay prepared for wasting infront of me, the field of battle had taken shape and the soldiers were ready to sell their lives as was to be decided by the fates. The legends had come to the point that they were no longer just legends, they had stepped over the line into reality, and now I face the enemy as ready and as able as I could be, though not certain of the outcome. I like many generals looked out over the situation as it developed and I like them had my doubts and my concerns. I knew the weaknesses of my forces and had some idea of the strengths laid against me. I could now only wait for the time to come, the battle to be engaged, and react as I saw fit to each eventuality or twist in the coming conflict.

Nailstead's second wave of troops scattered fully across the plain to either side of the black road. The time had arrived to strike. High up above the milling enemy circled my sky warriors. My divided forces to left and right waited hidden in a mist of wafting invisibility. Occasionally, I noted, with concern that the mists created to hide my troops wavered too much providing brief glimpses of the concealed, which worryingly became visible to me. Would Nailstead wherever he had chosen to create his command post, also be able to see the hidden defenders. Would he realise the trap into which he was thrusting his forces, had he got a counter ploy, was he expecting a maneuver of this kind from me, did he have a defense?

Like all generals charged with commanding armies, I worried that my planning might fail. I like previous combatants understood that the initial placement of forces depended on stealth; I believed that I had succeeded in that aim, now it only remained to wait for the exactly perfect second to engage and hopefully decimate my enemy. I intend to sweep my troops in from either side, whilst my enemies attention was diverted by the aerial force's attack. With their attention fixated high above my soldiers should be able to close, encircle and destroy, Nailstead's dark minions. Many would die this afternoon, but if luck was with me, I should, according to my plan emerge the victor.

 

High in the mountains, concealed from all sight, the coven had formed, the witch prepared the crystal, formed it into a binding entity with one aim in its existence.

I knew this; it concerned me because of the potential for this deep magic to become a threat to all life, not just my intended enemy. I had finally agreed to the stone being activated, but under well meant duress from both the witch and council of elders, still the thought of its awesome power being released scared me witless.

They truly believed that it would be possible to create a permanent place of restraint. To lock the minions of Nailstead out from this world, in to the dark of another, thus ending this war and all future wars. They argued that I must consider two things. The first was if I lost the battle then what would remain to fight the dark one? What force could quell his intentions and destroy his ambitions? They believed that nothing could, unless the crystal was formed unless it was allowed to fulfill its ancient promise.

Secondly they argued; what would I do with the prisoners were I to win? Would it be a massacre, would our people take the bloody road to damnation and destroy every living creature that had opposed us? They could never be allowed to roam free; they could not be tolerated to regroup, for in time they would find another leader if Nailstead had been removed, and then, we as a people would be facing their evil intent once more. That was a situation I could not allow to happen, they were right on both counts but still I had my doubts about setting free such ancient magic on my pleasant peaceful lands. Once free it might remain free and then what would be the outcome for all who just wanted to live their lives.

They had made valid points; I had listened intently keeping my doubts to my self. That was the time that I realized that I had not thought much past the impending confrontation. I, the King of this world had failed to think ahead, they the council to my kingship had not. The witch was charged with construction of the crystal, forming it initially as either an ultimate defense against defeat, or as a container to house those that had been defeated. I had agreed reluctantly but there had been no other choice once the future had been considered. I knew nothing of the witches true intent of the power she intended to claim of the nature of her desires.
